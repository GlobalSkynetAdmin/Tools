Grilled Chicken & Strawberry Spinach Salad
Fresh strawberries, candied walnuts, feta, and a sweet balsamic glaze
25 minPrep
~480Cal
44gProtein
Ingredients (serves 4)
  • 2 lbs chicken breasts (grilled) or 1 rotisserie chicken, sliced
  • 10 oz fresh baby spinach
  • 2 cups fresh strawberries, hulled and sliced
  • ½ cup crumbled feta cheese
  • ½ cup walnuts (toast in a dry pan 3–4 min, sprinkle with 1 tsp sugar while warm)
  • ¼ red onion, very thinly sliced
  • 3 tbsp balsamic vinegar, 2 tbsp olive oil, 1 tsp honey, pinch of salt
How to Make
  1. If grilling: season chicken with salt, pepper, olive oil. Grill over medium-high 6–7 min per side. Rest 5 min, slice thin.
  2. Toast walnuts in a dry skillet over medium heat, stirring constantly, 3–4 min until fragrant. Sprinkle with 1 tsp sugar in last 30 seconds — they'll get a light candy coating. Spread on parchment to cool.
  3. Whisk balsamic, olive oil, honey, and salt into a simple glaze dressing.
  4. Pile spinach in a large bowl. Add strawberries, red onion, feta, cooled walnuts.
  5. Lay sliced chicken over the top. Drizzle the balsamic glaze. Serve immediately — spinach wilts fast once dressed.
  6. The candied walnut trick takes 2 extra minutes and makes this feel like a restaurant salad.

~480 cal
44g protein
24g fat
26g carbs

Chicken · Fruit
Mango & Avocado Chicken Salad
Juicy mango, creamy avocado, and a honey-lime chili dressing — bright tropical flavor
20 minPrep
~510Cal
42gProtein
Ingredients (serves 4)
  • Rotisserie chicken — pull breast and thigh, shred or chop
  • 2 heads Romaine, chopped
  • 2 ripe mangoes, diced (or 1 bag frozen mango chunks, thawed and diced)
  • 2 avocados, diced
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • ¼ red onion, minced fine
  • Fresh cilantro, small handful (optional — skip if the family doesn't like it)
  • 3 tbsp lime juice, 2 tbsp olive oil, 1 tsp honey, ¼ tsp chili powder, pinch of salt
How to Make
  1. Whisk lime juice, olive oil, honey, chili powder, and salt into a dressing. Taste — should be bright, slightly sweet, little heat.
  2. Dice mango and avocado. Toss avocado with a squeeze of lime to keep it from browning.
  3. Arrange Romaine in bowls. Top with mango, avocado, tomatoes, red onion.
  4. Add shredded rotisserie chicken on top.
  5. Drizzle the honey-lime dressing over everything. Scatter cilantro if using.
  6. Frozen mango from Costco works perfectly here and is way cheaper than fresh — just thaw it the night before.

~510 cal

Wednesday
Vegetarian · Fruit
Watermelon, Feta & Mint Salad
The ultimate summer salad — sweet, salty, cooling, and shockingly filling with chickpeas
15 minPrep
~420Cal
18gProtein
Ingredients (serves 4)
  • ½ small seedless watermelon — cubed into bite-sized pieces (about 6 cups)
  • This is a fruit-first salad — the watermelon IS the base
  • 5 oz arugula or spring mix
  • 1 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1 can chickpeas, drained, rinsed, and patted dry
  • Small bunch fresh mint, leaves torn
  • ¼ red onion, sliced paper thin
  • 3 tbsp olive oil, 2 tbsp lime juice, 1 tsp honey, salt, black pepper
  • Optional: 1 English cucumber, sliced
How to Make
  1. Pat chickpeas completely dry with paper towels. Toss with 1 tbsp olive oil and a pinch of salt. Roast at 400°F for 20–25 min until crispy — this is key, soft chickpeas are sad, crispy chickpeas are great.
  2. While chickpeas roast, cube the watermelon. Refrigerate if you want it extra cold at serving.
  3. Whisk olive oil, lime juice, honey, salt, and a crack of black pepper for dressing.
  4. Lay arugula in a wide bowl or platter. Add watermelon, cucumber if using, red onion, and mint leaves.
  5. Scatter crispy chickpeas and feta over the top. Drizzle dressing. Serve immediately.
  6. Don't dress this ahead — watermelon releases a lot of juice fast. Build it and eat it.

~420 cal

Thursday
Grill · Chicken · Fruit
Grilled Peach & Chicken Salad
Charcoal-grilled peaches get caramelized and smoky — serve over arugula with goat cheese
30 minPrep
~490Cal
42gProtein
Ingredients (serves 4)
  • 2 lbs chicken thighs (boneless, skinless) — thighs stay juicier on the grill
  • 4 ripe but firm peaches, halved and pitted (firm = they hold up on the grill)
  • 5 oz arugula or spring mix
  • ½ cup crumbled goat cheese (or feta if easier to find)
  • ¼ cup pecans or sliced almonds
  • 3 tbsp balsamic vinegar, 3 tbsp olive oil, 1 tsp honey, 1 tsp Dijon mustard, salt, pepper
How to Make
  1. Light the charcoal. Season chicken thighs with salt, pepper, and olive oil. Grill 5–6 min per side over medium-high heat. Rest 5 min.
  2. While chicken rests: brush peach halves with a little olive oil and honey. Grill cut-side down over direct heat 3–4 min until you get grill marks and the flesh softens slightly. Don't move them — let them caramelize. Flip 1 min. Remove and slice into wedges.
  3. Whisk balsamic, olive oil, honey, Dijon, salt, and pepper into a vinaigrette.
  4. Arrange arugula in bowls. Slice chicken thin and lay over the greens.
  5. Add grilled peach wedges, scatter goat cheese and pecans. Drizzle balsamic vinaigrette generously.
  6. Grilled fruit is a genuine revelation if you haven't done it. The smoke + caramelized sugar is something else.

~490 cal

Chicken · Fruit
Apple Crunch BBQ Chicken Salad
Rotisserie in BBQ sauce, crisp apple, candied pecans, and creamy honey-mustard
15 minPrep
~530Cal
44gProtein
Ingredients (serves 4)
  • Rotisserie chicken — shred generously, warm in a pan with ¼ cup BBQ sauce
  • 2 heads Romaine, chopped
  • 2 large Honeycrisp or Fuji apples, cored and thinly sliced
  • ½ cup shredded cheddar
  • ½ cup pecans (toast with 1 tsp sugar same as the Monday walnut trick)
  • ¼ red onion, sliced thin
  • Dressing: 3 tbsp honey mustard, 2 tbsp apple cider vinegar, 2 tbsp olive oil, salt
How to Make
  1. Warm shredded rotisserie chicken in a skillet with BBQ sauce, 2–3 min on medium. It should be sticky and a little caramelized on the edges.
  2. Toast pecans with a touch of sugar in a dry skillet — 3 min, watch carefully. Cool on parchment.
  3. Whisk honey mustard, apple cider vinegar, olive oil, and salt into a dressing.
  4. Slice apples thin just before serving — toss with a little lemon juice to prevent browning.
  5. Assemble: Romaine, apples, cheddar, red onion, candied pecans, warm BBQ chicken on top. Drizzle honey-mustard dressing.
  6. Apple + BBQ + honey-mustard is one of those combinations that shouldn't work as well as it does. It really works.

Saturday
Chicken · Berry
Blueberry Chicken & Quinoa Power Salad
Antioxidant-loaded and genuinely filling — blueberries, quinoa, spinach, almonds
25 minPrep
~520Cal
46gProtein
Ingredients (serves 4)
  • Rotisserie chicken — pull breast meat, slice
  • 1 cup dry quinoa (makes ~3 cups cooked) — rinse before cooking
  • 8 oz baby spinach
  • 1½ cups fresh or frozen blueberries (thaw if frozen)
  • ½ cup sliced almonds
  • ½ cup crumbled feta
  • Dressing: 3 tbsp lemon juice, 2 tbsp olive oil, 1 tbsp honey, 1 tsp Dijon mustard, salt, pepper
How to Make
  1. Cook quinoa: 1 cup quinoa to 2 cups water, bring to boil, reduce to simmer, cover 15 min. Fluff with fork. Let cool to warm — not hot or it'll wilt the spinach.
  2. Toast sliced almonds in a dry skillet 2–3 min, shaking constantly. They go from toasted to burnt fast.
  3. Whisk lemon juice, olive oil, honey, Dijon, salt, and pepper into dressing.
  4. In a large bowl, combine warm quinoa and spinach — the heat will lightly wilt the spinach just slightly, which is perfect.
  5. Add blueberries, sliced chicken, feta, and toasted almonds. Drizzle dressing and toss gently.
  6. This one holds up better than most dressed salads — quinoa keeps it from going soggy. Leftovers are good the next day.
Nutrition Estimate
~520 cal
46g protein

Sunday
Chicken · Simple
Big Caprese Chicken Salad
Tomato, fresh mozzarella, basil, balsamic glaze — Italian simplicity at its best
15 minPrep
~470Cal
46gProtein
Ingredients (serves 4)
  • Rotisserie chicken — pull and slice or shred whatever's left, or grab a fresh bird
  • 5 oz spring mix or arugula
  • 2 cups cherry tomatoes, halved (or 3 large tomatoes, sliced)
  • 8 oz fresh mozzarella — the ball kind, sliced (find in the deli section at Macey's)
  • Large handful fresh basil leaves
  • 3 tbsp good balsamic vinegar, 2 tbsp olive oil, salt, cracked black pepper
  • Optional: balsamic glaze (thick reduction — Macey's usually stocks it near the vinegar)
How to Make
  1. Slice fresh mozzarella, halve tomatoes, tear basil leaves. These three are the heart of it — buy the best versions Macey's has.
  2. Lay spring mix on a large serving platter — go wide, not deep. This is a platter salad.
  3. Arrange tomatoes and mozzarella slices over the greens, alternating and overlapping.
  4. Lay sliced rotisserie chicken over the top.
  5. Scatter torn basil leaves. Drizzle olive oil, then balsamic. Hit with good salt and cracked pepper.
  6. Drizzle balsamic glaze over everything at the end if you have it — it's the finishing touch that makes this look spectacular.
  7. Sunday night, don't stress. This is the easiest salad of the week and possibly the most impressive-looking one.
Nutrition Estimate
~470 cal
46g protein
26g fat
14g carbs

Summer Drink Lineup
All kid-friendly, low-calorie, and built around the same fruit you're already buying for the salads. Zero waste, maximum fun.
🍓
Strawberry Lemonade Sparkler
Pairs with Monday's strawberry spinach salad perfectly
Kid Favorite ~55 cal Best with: Mon, Fri
Recipe (serves 4)
  • 1 cup fresh strawberries, blended smooth
  • ½ cup fresh lemon juice (~4 lemons)
  • 2 tbsp honey
  • 2 cups sparkling water
  • 1 cup cold water
  • Ice, fresh strawberry slices to garnish
Blend strawberries with a splash of water until completely smooth. Strain through a fine mesh strainer. Combine with lemon juice, honey, and cold water. Stir well. Pour over ice, top with a splash of sparkling water right before serving. The bubbles make it feel special. Kids will ask for this every night once you make it once.
🥭
Mango Lime Agua Fresca
Bright, tropical, and zero-stress to make
Kid Friendly ~40 cal Best with: Tue, Wed
Recipe (serves 4)
  • 2 cups frozen mango chunks (thawed) — same bag as Tuesday's salad
  • Juice of 2 limes
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • 4 cups cold water
  • Pinch of salt
  • Ice, lime wheels to serve
Blend thawed mango with 1 cup water until silky smooth. Strain into a pitcher. Add remaining water, lime juice, honey, and a pinch of salt. Stir well and refrigerate. The pinch of salt is what makes it taste like restaurant agua fresca rather than watered-down mango juice. Uses the exact same frozen mango as Tuesday's salad — zero extra shopping.
🫐
Blueberry Mint Lemonade
Deep purple, gorgeous color, antioxidant powerhouse
Kid Favorite ~50 cal Best with: Sat, Sun
Recipe (serves 4)
  • 1 cup blueberries — same bag as Saturday's salad
  • ½ cup fresh lemon juice
  • 2 tbsp honey
  • 4 cups cold water
  • 6–8 fresh mint leaves
  • Ice
Muddle (smash) blueberries in the bottom of a pitcher with the mint leaves and honey until the blueberries release their juice. Add lemon juice and cold water. Stir well and taste — add more honey if you want it sweeter. Strain out the blueberry skins if you want it smooth, or leave them for a more rustic look. The color is a deep violet purple that kids absolutely love. Make it in a clear pitcher so everyone can see it.
🍵
Cold Brew Mint Green Tea
Make Sunday night — drinks itself all week from the fridge
~0 cal Pairs with everything Family Staple
Recipe (makes 1 pitcher)
  • 5 green tea bags
  • 1 liter cold water (not hot — cold brew method)
  • Small bunch fresh mint
  • Optional: 1–2 tbsp honey to sweeten
  • Lemon slices to serve
Drop tea bags and mint into a pitcher of cold water. Refrigerate overnight — minimum 6 hours, ideally 8–12. Remove bags and mint. Sweeten with honey if desired. That's it. Cold-brewing green tea produces a much smoother, less bitter flavor than hot-brewing. It's so easy that once you start doing this you'll keep a pitcher going all summer. Zero calories if you skip the honey, barely any if you don't.
🍑
Peach Ginger Lemonade
Warm weather in a glass — pairs beautifully with the grilled peach salad
Kid Friendly ~45 cal Best with: Thu, Fri
Recipe (serves 4)
  • 2 ripe peaches, chopped (or ½ cup peach jam as a shortcut)
  • ½ cup lemon juice
  • 1 tsp fresh ginger, grated (or ¼ tsp ground ginger)
  • 2 tbsp honey
  • 4 cups cold water
  • Ice, peach slices to garnish
If using fresh peaches: blend with a splash of water until smooth, strain. Combine with lemon juice, grated ginger, honey, and cold water. Taste and adjust — the ginger should be a background note, not the star. If using peach jam: whisk 2–3 tbsp jam directly into the lemon water with ginger and honey — easier and works just as well. Serve over ice.
🍉
Watermelon Lime Cooler
Made from Wednesday's watermelon — literally no extra shopping
Kid Absolute Favorite ~35 cal Best with: Wed, Thu
Recipe (serves 4)
  • 4 cups watermelon chunks — cubed from Wednesday's salad watermelon
  • Juice of 2 limes
  • Pinch of salt
  • 1 cup sparkling water (optional but great)
  • Ice, lime wheels, fresh mint
Blend watermelon until smooth — it blends almost instantly. Strain through a fine strainer to remove pulp. Add lime juice and a pinch of salt. Serve over ice with a splash of sparkling water if you want it lighter and more refreshing. This is the simplest drink on the list and consistently the biggest hit with kids. Buy a slightly larger watermelon Wednesday and save a quarter of it specifically for this drink.

Buy slightly more of each fruit than the salad needs and you have drinks covered for the whole week. One list. One trip.

The fruit-forward nature of this week actually makes prep faster — most of the "work" is washing and slicing, not cooking. Sunday prep sets you up for the full week in about 30 minutes flat.

Sunday Evening Batch Prep
30 minutes that makes the whole week feel effortless
~30 min
🥬
Wash and dry all greens. Spinach bags (Mon + Sat), arugula/spring mix bags (Wed, Thu, Sun), and Romaine hearts (Tue, Fri). Dry completely, store each in a zip-lock with a paper towel. This is the single most important prep step.
🫐
Set frozen mango and blueberries to thaw in the fridge. Put them in bowls Sunday night so they're ready Tuesday and Saturday. Frozen fruit is perfectly fine and often better quality than off-season fresh.
🫙
Mix the dressings that keep. Make Monday's balsamic glaze dressing, Thursday's balsamic-Dijon vinaigrette, and Saturday's lemon-honey dressing now. Store in small jars in the fridge. They last 1–2 weeks. Shake before using.
🍵
Start the cold brew green tea. Drop 5 green tea bags and a handful of mint into a pitcher of cold water. Refrigerate overnight. By Monday evening you have a pitcher of cold brew tea ready for the week.
🌾
Cook quinoa for Saturday. Rinse 1 cup quinoa, cook in 2 cups water — boil, cover, simmer 15 min. Cool completely, store in the fridge. Cooked quinoa keeps 5 days refrigerated and Saturday's prep time drops to under 10 min.
🥜
Candy the nuts for the week. Toast walnuts (Mon) and pecans (Thu, Fri) in the same pan with a sprinkle of sugar. Do them all at once Sunday so you're not doing the same skillet trick three nights in a row. Store cooled nuts in a small jar — they keep at room temp all week.
Fruit Handling Guide
How to buy, ripen, and time each fruit right
Reference
🍓
Strawberries (Monday): Buy the day of or day before. They go fast. Don't wash until you're ready to use them — moisture accelerates mold. Hull and slice right before serving.
🥭
Mangoes and avocados (Tuesday): Buy avocados firm Sunday — they'll be perfectly ripe by Tuesday. Buy mangoes slightly soft. If using frozen mango, thaw in the fridge overnight Monday.
🍉
Watermelon (Wednesday): Buy the smallest seedless you can find — you don't need a whole giant melon. Cube what you need for the salad, save the rest for the Watermelon Lime Cooler. Refrigerate cut watermelon in a sealed container.
🍑
Peaches (Thursday): Buy firm but fragrant. Leave them on the counter — don't refrigerate unripe peaches. You want them firm enough to hold grill marks but ripe enough to caramelize. Smell them at Macey's — a good peach smells like a peach.
🍎
Apples (Friday): Honeycrisp or Fuji — buy whichever looks best. Slice right before serving and toss with a little lemon juice to prevent browning. These keep easily in the fridge all week — no timing pressure.
🫐
Blueberries (Saturday): If using frozen, pull them out Saturday morning to thaw. If fresh, rinse gently right before using. Fresh or frozen both work perfectly in this salad — the quinoa is the star anyway.
Thursday Grill Night Notes
Getting grilled fruit right the first time
Grill Tips
🔥
Light the charcoal 25 min before dinner. You want a medium-hot fire for this one — slightly lower than steak temp. Peaches burn fast on super-high heat.
🍑
Brush peaches with olive oil and honey right before grilling. Cut side down on the grill, no peeking for 3–4 minutes. You need the grill mark contact to caramelize the sugar. They'll release naturally when they're ready.
🍗
Cook chicken thighs first, then peaches while chicken rests. Thighs need 5–6 min per side. Slide them to indirect heat while you grill the peaches. Everything hits the table at the same time with no reheating needed.
⚡
Don't overcomplicate it. Grilled fruit sounds fancy but it's genuinely one of the easiest things you can do on a grill. If a peach half falls apart, just slice it and put it on anyway — it'll still taste incredible.
🍓 Working with Fresh Fruit in Salads
  • Always add fruit right before serving — it releases juice fast once cut.
  • Toss sliced apples, avocado, and peaches with a squeeze of lemon to prevent browning.
  • Slightly underripe fruit holds up better in salads than very soft fruit — it won't turn mushy.
  • Frozen fruit (mango, blueberries) is often superior to off-season fresh — picked at peak ripeness and frozen immediately.
  • Wash strawberries right before using, not before storing — moisture speeds up mold.
  • Watermelon: buy firm, cube cold, serve immediately. Don't let it sit in the bowl.
💪 Nutrition Notes for Training
  • The fruit carbs in these salads are ideal for post-workout — fast-digesting natural sugars replenish glycogen quickly.
  • Wednesday's vegetarian watermelon salad is lower in protein (18g) — add a can of chickpeas and a couple of hard-boiled eggs if you need more.
  • Saturday's blueberry quinoa is your highest-antioxidant meal of the week — great timing if Saturday is a heavy training day.
  • Rotisserie chicken thigh meat has more fat and flavor than breast — both are excellent protein sources.
  • The honey in dressings is a feature, not a flaw — small amounts of natural sugar help fat-soluble vitamins absorb from the salad greens.
🛒 Eureka Shopping Notes
  • Summer produce quality at Macey's is genuinely good — check the strawberries and peaches in person before buying.
  • Costco's frozen fruit section is excellent and a fraction of the cost of fresh off-season fruit.
  • Watermelon: one small seedless does the job — you don't need a full-size one for a family of 4.
  • Fresh mozzarella is usually in the deli section at Macey's, not with regular cheese — ask if you can't find it.
  • Goat cheese: check both the regular cheese section and the deli. If Macey's is out, feta makes a perfect substitute in every recipe.
  • Quinoa: look in the grain/rice aisle. It cooks exactly like rice — don't let it intimidate you.
🔄 Easy Swaps
  • No goat cheese? Feta works in every single recipe this week.
  • Kids don't like arugula (too peppery)? Swap for spring mix or Romaine in any recipe.
  • Cilantro skeptics: just leave it out — the Tuesday mango salad is great either way.
  • Quinoa takes 15 min — if you're short on time Saturday, skip it and double the spinach. Still excellent.
  • No peaches available? Nectarines grill identically and taste almost the same.
  • Wednesday vegetarian not filling enough? Add grilled halloumi cheese or a pan of crispy chickpeas to boost it significantly.
