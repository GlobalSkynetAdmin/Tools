Monday
Rotisserie Chicken Caesar
Classic Caesar — zero cooking, just pull and plate
🍗 Rotisserie
10 minPrep
~500Cal
46gProtein
Ingredients (serves 4)
  • ½ rotisserie chicken — pull breast meat, slice or shred
  • No raw chicken to cook — just pull it apart at serving time
  • 2 large heads Romaine lettuce, chopped
  • 1 cup shredded Parmesan
  • 1½ cups croutons
  • ½ cup Caesar dressing (bottled)
  • Black pepper, optional squeeze of lemon
How to Make
  1. Pull breast and thigh meat from the rotisserie chicken. Slice or shred — whatever your family prefers.
  2. Chop Romaine and spin or pat completely dry (wet lettuce = watery dressing).
  3. Toss Romaine with Caesar dressing in a large bowl. Start light, taste, add more.
  4. Plate up, top with chicken, Parmesan, croutons, fresh black pepper.
  5. Rotisserie chicken is already seasoned — go easy on any extra salt.

Tuesday
Charcoal-Grilled Steak & Roasted Veggie Salad
Strip steak off the charcoal grill over sweet potato and arugula
🔥 Charcoal Grill
35 minPrep
~610Cal
44gProtein
Ingredients (serves 4)
  • 1½–2 lbs strip steak or sirloin
  • 2 medium sweet potatoes, cubed ½ inch
  • 5 oz arugula or spring mix
  • 1 red onion, sliced into rings
  • ½ cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 3 tbsp balsamic vinegar, 3 tbsp olive oil, 1 tsp honey
  • Salt, pepper, garlic powder
How to Make
  1. Light the charcoal about 25–30 min before you want to cook. You want a hot two-zone fire — coals banked to one side.
  2. Preheat oven to 425°F. Toss sweet potato cubes with olive oil, salt, pepper. Roast 20–22 min, flipping halfway, while the coals get ready.
  3. Season steak generously with salt, pepper, garlic powder. Let it sit at room temp while the coals ash over.
  4. Grill steak over direct high heat, 3–4 min per side for medium (130–135°F). Move to indirect side if flare-ups happen. Rest 8 min off the grill — this is non-negotiable for juicy steak.
  5. Whisk balsamic, olive oil, honey, pinch of salt for dressing.
  6. Arrange arugula, roasted sweet potato, red onion rings. Slice steak thin against the grain, lay over the top, crumble feta, drizzle dressing.
  7. Charcoal tip: lump charcoal burns hotter and cleaner than briquettes — worth it for steak.

Wednesday
Southwest Taco Salad
Seasoned ground beef, black beans, corn, creamy lime dressing
25 minPrep
~580Cal
42gProtein
Ingredients (serves 4)
  • 1½ lbs 80/20 ground beef
  • 1 packet taco seasoning
  • 1 head Romaine lettuce, chopped
  • 1 can black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can sweet corn, drained
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1 cup sour cream + juice of 1 lime + pinch of salt (your dressing)
  • Tortilla chips, optional salsa or jalapeño
How to Make
  1. Brown ground beef in a skillet over medium-high, breaking it up. Drain excess fat.
  2. Add taco seasoning + 2–3 tbsp water. Stir and simmer 2–3 min. Take off heat.
  3. Mix sour cream with lime juice and a pinch of salt — this is the dressing.
  4. Build: Romaine base, beans, corn, cheddar, warm beef on top.
  5. Drizzle lime-sour cream dressing. Crush tortilla chips over each bowl at the table.

Thursday
Salmon & Cucumber Dill Salad
Pan-seared salmon over crisp greens with lemon-dill vinaigrette
25 minPrep
~490Cal
46gProtein
Ingredients (serves 4)
  • 4 salmon fillets (5–6 oz each)
  • 1 large English cucumber, sliced thin
  • 5 oz spring mix or butter lettuce
  • ½ red onion, sliced thin
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 3 tbsp olive oil, juice of 2 lemons
  • 1 tbsp dried dill, 1 tsp Dijon mustard, salt, pepper
How to Make
  1. Pat salmon dry. Season with salt, pepper, squeeze of lemon.
  2. Heat olive oil in a non-stick skillet over medium-high. Sear skin-side up 3–4 min, flip, cook 3 more min. Salmon should flake easily at the thickest part.
  3. Whisk together olive oil, lemon juice, Dijon, dill, salt, pepper for dressing.
  4. Arrange greens, cucumber, tomatoes, red onion in bowls.
  5. Place salmon on top, skin-side down. Drizzle dressing generously.
  6. Frozen salmon works great here — thaw overnight in the fridge.

Friday
BBQ Rotisserie & Bacon Chopped Salad
Shredded rotisserie tossed in warm BBQ sauce — smoky, fast, crowd-pleasing
🍗 Rotisserie
15 minPrep
~540Cal
48gProtein
Ingredients (serves 4)
  • ½–¾ rotisserie chicken — shred thigh and breast meat
  • ½ cup BBQ sauce
  • 6 strips thick-cut bacon
  • 2 heads Romaine, chopped fine (this is a chopped salad — small pieces)
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar
  • 1 can sweet corn, drained
  • ½ cup ranch dressing
  • 2 green onions, sliced
How to Make
  1. Cook bacon in a skillet until crisp. Set on paper towels, crumble when cool.
  2. Shred rotisserie chicken (thigh meat especially — more flavor). Warm briefly in a skillet with the BBQ sauce, just 2–3 min to get it sticky and hot.
  3. Finely chop the Romaine — smaller pieces than a regular salad make this work.
  4. Combine Romaine, corn, cheddar, bacon in a bowl and toss.
  5. Top with warm BBQ chicken, drizzle ranch, scatter green onions over everything.

Saturday
Greek Salad with Rotisserie Chicken
Feta, olives, cucumber, tomato — bright and easy
🍗 Rotisserie
15 minPrep
~460Cal
42gProtein
Ingredients (serves 4)
  • Remaining rotisserie chicken — pull whatever is left, chop rough
  • 1 English cucumber, chopped
  • 2 cups cherry tomatoes, halved
  • ½ red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup kalamata olives, halved
  • 1 cup crumbled feta
  • 5 oz Romaine or spring mix
  • 4 tbsp olive oil, 2 tbsp red wine vinegar, 1 tsp dried oregano, salt, pepper
How to Make
  1. Whisk together olive oil, red wine vinegar, oregano, salt, pepper into a simple vinaigrette.
  2. Combine cucumber, tomatoes, red onion, olives. Toss with half the dressing. Let sit 5 min.
  3. Arrange greens, add marinated veggies, lay chopped rotisserie chicken on top.
  4. Crumble feta generously. Drizzle remaining dressing. Done.
  5. If you're short on chicken from the week, grab a second rotisserie bird at Costco — or this is a great night for a fresh one.

Sunday
Cobb Salad
Classic Cobb — egg-optional for anyone who prefers without
🍗 Rotisserie
20 minPrep
~530Cal
46gProtein
Ingredients (serves 4)
  • Rotisserie chicken — shred or chop generously
  • 6 large eggs — hard boiled (see egg note below)
  • Egg-optional: serve eggs on the side so everyone builds their own bowl. Your wife can skip, you can pile them on — fresh-boiled eggs may actually win her over.
  • 6 strips thick-cut bacon, cooked and crumbled
  • 2 heads Romaine, chopped
  • 2 avocados, diced
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • ½ cup blue cheese crumbles (or feta)
  • ½ cup ranch or blue cheese dressing
How to Make
  1. Hard boil eggs if using: cover with cold water, bring to full boil, cook 10 min, transfer to ice water 5 min, peel. Slice just before serving.
  2. Cook bacon until crisp. Crumble when cool.
  3. Shred or chop rotisserie chicken.
  4. Dice avocado right before serving — toss with a little lemon juice to prevent browning.
  5. Arrange Romaine in a wide serving bowl or platter. Lay toppings in rows — classic Cobb presentation — then let everyone build their own plate.
  6. Serve dressing on the side. Put the egg bowl on the side too — no pressure, no arguments.
  7. Fresh-boiled, properly cooked eggs (not rubbery) are a genuinely different experience than old diner eggs — worth trying one before writing them off.
Nutrition Estimate
~530 cal
46g protein
34g fat
14g carbs

Every-Night Standbys
Kid-friendly, low-calorie, and actually refreshing. Keep ingredients stocked so you can mix one up any night.
🍋
Sparkling Lemon-Mint Water
The house drink — elegant, zero-calorie, infinitely refillable
Kid Friendly ~0 cal Pairs with: Everything
Recipe (per pitcher)
  • 1 liter sparkling water (LaCroix, Bubly, or store brand)
  • 1 lemon, sliced thin
  • Small handful fresh mint leaves
  • Ice
Combine in a pitcher and let it sit 10 min before serving. The mint and lemon infuse into the bubbles. Kids love it, it pairs with everything, and it looks fancier than it is. Costco sells bulk sparkling water — keep a case on hand all summer.
🍓
Strawberry Basil Lemonade
Fresh, slightly sweet, bright pink — kids go wild for it
Kid Friendly ~60 cal Pairs with: Caesar, Greek, Cobb
Recipe (serves 4)
  • 1 cup fresh or frozen strawberries
  • ½ cup fresh lemon juice (about 3–4 lemons)
  • 2 tbsp honey or sugar
  • 4 cups cold water
  • 4–5 fresh basil leaves
  • Ice
Blend strawberries with a splash of water until smooth. Strain if you want it clear. Combine with lemon juice, honey, water, and basil. Stir well, pour over ice. The basil sounds fancy but it's what makes it taste like a restaurant drink.
🥒
Cucumber Lime Agua Fresca
Light, cooling, slightly sweet — perfect for hot Utah evenings
Kid Friendly ~25 cal Pairs with: Taco, Salmon, Greek
Recipe (serves 4)
  • 1 large English cucumber, roughly chopped (use your salad cucumber)
  • Juice of 2 limes
  • 1 tbsp honey or agave
  • 4 cups cold water
  • Pinch of salt
  • Ice, lime slices to serve
Blend cucumber with 1 cup of water until smooth. Strain through a fine mesh strainer into a pitcher. Add remaining water, lime juice, honey, pinch of salt. Taste and adjust. Serve over ice with a lime slice. Uses the same cucumber you're buying for salads — zero waste.
🍵
Peach Iced Green Tea
Antioxidant-rich, lightly sweet, beautiful amber color
Kid Friendly ~30 cal Pairs with: Steak, BBQ Chicken, Cobb
Recipe (makes 1 pitcher)
  • 4 green tea bags
  • 4 cups boiling water
  • 2 cups cold water
  • 2–3 tbsp peach jam or preserves (Macey's will have it)
  • Ice, peach slices optional
Steep tea bags 3–4 min in boiling water. Remove bags, stir in peach jam while hot. Add cold water. Refrigerate until cold or pour over ice. The jam trick sounds weird but it works — it dissolves perfectly and tastes like real peach tea. Make a pitcher Sunday night for the whole week.

Steak Night Optional Treat

Salmon Night Light
Recommended Drinks

Friday BBQ Night
Smoky, sweet BBQ needs something to cut through it
BBQ Night Kid Friendly
Recommended Drinks
  • Best match: Strawberry basil lemonade — the acidity cuts the BBQ sauce perfectly
  • Alt: Sparkling water with a splash of apple juice and ice — kids love it, looks fancy

Friday is your easiest cooking night. Don't overthink the drink. Make the strawberry lemonade if you have time, grab a sparkling water if you don't.

If you do want one adult drink on a special night — steak Tuesday or salmon Thursday — a single glass of a dry red or white is roughly 120–130 cal and pairs genuinely well. That's a far cry from derailing anything. Just something to know it's in your pocket if you want it.

With rotisserie chicken, Sunday prep gets even simpler — no raw chicken to cook. The whole session is now about greens, eggs, and getting your drinks ready for the week.

Sunday Batch Prep
30 minutes of work that makes the entire week easier
~25–30 min
🥬
Wash and dry all greens. Both Romaine and the arugula/spring mix bag. Spin completely dry. Store in zip-lock bags with a paper towel inside — this one step is the difference between crisp salads and soggy ones mid-week.
🧅
Slice red onions, then quick-pickle them. Slice both onions thin, put in a jar, cover with red wine vinegar and a pinch of salt. Ready in 30 min, better after a few hours. Mellows the raw bite dramatically — your family will actually eat them.
🍠
Roast sweet potatoes for Tuesday. Cube, toss with olive oil and salt, roast at 425°F for 20 min. Store in the fridge — reheat in a dry skillet Tuesday before building the steak salad.
🥚
Hard boil eggs for Sunday's Cobb — or wait until Sunday night. If you do them now: cover with cold water, boil 10 min, ice bath 5 min, refrigerate unpeeled. Peel right before serving. Fresh-boiled eggs really are a different experience — worth offering them on the side and letting everyone decide.
🫙
Mix the dressings that keep. Make the lemon-dill vinaigrette (Thursday) and the Greek vinaigrette (Saturday) now — shake together in small mason jars and refrigerate. Shake before using. Vinaigrettes last 1–2 weeks.
🍵
Brew a pitcher of peach iced green tea. Takes 15 min, lasts all week in the fridge. Best drink you can have cold waiting for you every night.
Rotisserie Chicken Strategy
How to make 2 birds stretch across 4 meals cleanly
Reference
🍗
Bird #1 (Monday + Friday): Monday night, pull breast meat for the Caesar — you'll use about half the breast. Friday, shred thigh meat for the BBQ chopped salad. Thigh meat shreds better and holds up to BBQ sauce. Refrigerate carcass in a bag between meals.
🍗
Bird #2 (Saturday + Sunday): Saturday, pull breast and thigh for the Greek salad — chop it rough. Sunday, use whatever remains for the Cobb. Shred or slice depending on what you have left. If one bird looks thin, a Costco rotisserie is so cheap it's worth picking up a third on the same trip just to have insurance.
🦴
Bonus: make stock with the carcasses. If you're into that kind of thing — toss both carcasses in a pot with water, onion, celery, carrot, salt, simmer 2 hours. Freeze it. Free chicken stock for soups all summer. Totally optional but wildly useful.
Tuesday Charcoal Grill Notes
Getting the most out of the charcoal setup for a weeknight
Grill Tips
🔥
Light coals 25–30 min before you want to eat. Plan backwards from dinner time — charcoal isn't fast. A chimney starter is the best investment you can make for weeknight grilling: no lighter fluid, ready in 15 min.
🥩
Pull steak from the fridge 20–30 min before grilling. Room temperature meat sears better and cooks more evenly. Salt it generously as soon as it comes out — the salt starts working immediately.
🌡️
Target 130°F for medium. An instant-read thermometer is worth it — steak is too expensive to guess. Pull at 125°F and let carryover heat finish it during the 8-minute rest.
⏱️
Use the grill heat while you have it. While the steak rests, throw the sweet potato on the grill in foil if you didn't roast them in the oven Sunday. Or grill some corn for a bonus side.
🥬 Keeping Greens Fresh All Week
  • Wash and spin dry all greens Sunday before storing.
  • Paper towel in the zip-lock bag absorbs moisture — greens stay crisp 5–6 days.
  • Never dress greens ahead of time — always right before serving.
  • Arugula wilts faster than Romaine — use it by Thursday.
  • Limp greens? Soak in ice water 5 min. They crisp right back up.
💪 Supporting Your Training
  • Every salad here hits 40–50g protein per serving — solid for muscle recovery.
  • On heavy lift days (Mon, Thu, Fri) lean into higher-carb nights: Taco Salad, BBQ Chicken.
  • Salmon Thursday is ideal post-training — omega-3s actively reduce muscle inflammation.
  • Add a second portion of chicken or an extra hard-boiled egg to bump protein without changing the recipe.
  • The avocado in the Cobb is quality fat — leave it in, it supports hormone production and recovery.
🛒 Eureka Shopping Strategy
  • Hit Costco first (Spanish Fork), then Macey's on the way back through Santaquin — same highway, one trip.
  • Buy avocados firm — they ripen perfectly over 4–5 days and are ready by Sunday.
  • Stock extra canned goods (beans, corn, olives) and bottled dressings — they last forever and cut future trips.
  • Frozen salmon is completely fine if the fresh counter is thin — thaw overnight in the fridge.
  • Rotisserie chickens at Costco are around $5 — cheaper than raw chicken per pound of cooked meat.
🔄 Swaps & What You Might Be Missing
  • Salad dressing variety: Keep a second bottle of something different — Green Goddess, Thousand Island — so the family doesn't get bored of ranch and Caesar.
  • Bread on the side: A crusty loaf or some warm rolls makes salad feel like a full dinner to skeptical kids and hungry post-workout adults.
  • Protein powder: If you're tracking protein closely for lifting, a shake alongside dinner is an easy 25g bump that doesn't change the meal.
  • Don't forget salt and pepper grinders: Fresh-ground makes a real difference on salads — especially the steak and Caesar.
  • Paper towels: You'll go through them. The greens need them, the steak needs them, the bacon needs them.
