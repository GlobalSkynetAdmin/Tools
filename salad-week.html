Weekly Dinner Plan · Family of 3–4

The SaladWeek

Seven hearty, protein-rich salads built from one grocery trip. Every ingredient pulls double or triple duty across the week — so nothing goes to waste and you're never driving back to Santaquin mid-week.

Monday
Grilled Chicken Caesar
Classic done right — anchovy-free dressing, plenty of protein
25 minPrep
~520Cal / serving
48gProtein
Ingredients (serves 4)
  • 2 lbs boneless skinless chicken breasts
  • 2 large heads Romaine lettuce, chopped
  • 1 cup shredded Parmesan
  • 1½ cups croutons (store-bought or homemade)
  • ½ cup Caesar dressing (bottled is fine)
  • 1 tsp garlic powder, salt, pepper, olive oil
How to Make
  1. Season chicken with garlic powder, salt, pepper, drizzle of olive oil.
  2. Grill or pan-sear over medium-high heat, 6–7 min per side until cooked through (165°F). Rest 5 min, then slice.
  3. Chop Romaine and spin dry (or pat dry). Add to a large bowl.
  4. Toss with Caesar dressing — start with less, taste, add more.
  5. Top with sliced chicken, Parmesan, and croutons. Serve immediately.

~520 cal
48g protein

20g carbs
Tuesday
Steak & Roasted Veggie Salad
Strip steak over roasted sweet potato and arugula with balsamic
35 minPrep
~610Cal / serving
44gProtein
Ingredients (serves 4)
  • 1½ lbs strip steak or sirloin
  • 2 medium sweet potatoes, cubed ½ inch
  • 5 oz arugula (or spring mix)
  • 1 red onion, sliced into rings
  • ½ cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 3 tbsp balsamic vinegar, 3 tbsp olive oil, 1 tsp honey
  • Salt, pepper, garlic powder
How to Make
  1. Preheat oven to 425°F. Toss sweet potato cubes with olive oil, salt, pepper. Roast 20–22 min, flipping halfway.
  2. Season steak generously with salt, pepper, garlic powder. Let sit at room temp while veggies roast.
  3. Sear steak in cast iron or heavy pan over high heat, 3–4 min per side for medium. Rest 8 min, then slice thin against the grain.
  4. Whisk together balsamic, olive oil, honey, pinch of salt for dressing.
  5. Arrange arugula, roasted sweet potato, red onion. Top with steak, feta. Drizzle dressing.

~610 cal

Wednesday
Southwest Taco Salad
Seasoned ground beef, black beans, corn, with creamy lime dressing
25 minPrep
~580Cal / serving
42gProtein
Ingredients (serves 4)
  • 1½ lbs 80/20 ground beef
  • 1 packet taco seasoning (or: cumin, chili powder, garlic powder, oregano)
  • 1 head Romaine lettuce, chopped
  • 1 can black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can sweet corn, drained
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1 cup sour cream, juice of 1 lime, salt
  • Tortilla chips for topping
  • Optional: salsa, sliced jalapeño, avocado
How to Make
  1. Brown ground beef in a skillet over medium-high heat, breaking it up. Drain fat.
  2. Add taco seasoning + 2–3 tbsp water. Stir and simmer 2–3 min. Remove from heat.
  3. Mix sour cream with lime juice and a pinch of salt — this is your dressing.
  4. Build the salad: Romaine first, then beans, corn, cheese, warm beef.
  5. Drizzle lime-sour cream dressing over top. Crush tortilla chips over each serving.

~580 cal
42g protein

Thursday
Salmon & Cucumber Dill Salad
Pan-seared salmon over crisp greens with a lemon-dill vinaigrette
25 minPrep
~490Cal / serving
46gProtein
Ingredients (serves 4)
  • 4 salmon fillets (5–6 oz each)
  • 1 large English cucumber, sliced thin
  • 5 oz spring mix or butter lettuce
  • ½ red onion, sliced thin
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 3 tbsp olive oil, juice of 2 lemons
  • 1 tbsp dried dill (or 2 tbsp fresh), 1 tsp Dijon mustard, salt, pepper
How to Make
  1. Pat salmon dry, season with salt, pepper, a squeeze of lemon.
  2. Heat olive oil in a non-stick skillet over medium-high. Sear salmon skin-side up 3–4 min, flip, cook 3 more min. Fish should flake easily.
  3. Whisk together olive oil, lemon juice, Dijon, dill, salt, pepper for the dressing.
  4. Arrange greens, cucumber, tomatoes, red onion in bowls.
  5. Place salmon on top (skin side down so it stays crisp). Drizzle dressing generously.

~490 cal
46g protein

Friday
BBQ Chicken & Bacon Chopped Salad
Crowd-pleaser Friday — smoky, creamy, satisfying
25 minPrep
~560Cal / serving
50gProtein
Ingredients (serves 4)
  • 2 lbs chicken thighs (boneless skinless)
  • ½ cup BBQ sauce (any store brand)
  • 6 strips thick-cut bacon
  • 2 heads Romaine lettuce, chopped fine
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar
  • 1 can sweet corn, drained
  • ½ cup ranch dressing
  • 2 green onions, sliced
How to Make
  1. Cook bacon in a skillet until crisp. Set aside on paper towels, then crumble. Leave a little bacon fat in the pan.
  2. Season chicken thighs with salt and pepper. Cook in same skillet over medium-high heat 5–6 min per side until cooked through.
  3. Brush chicken liberally with BBQ sauce in last minute of cooking. Rest 5 min, then chop into bite-sized pieces.
  4. Finely chop the Romaine (this is a "chopped" salad — small pieces make it great).
  5. Toss greens, corn, cheddar, bacon together. Top with BBQ chicken, drizzle ranch, scatter green onions.

Saturday
Greek Salad with Grilled Chicken
Feta, olives, cucumber, tomato — bright and clean
20 minPrep
~470Cal / serving
44gProtein
Ingredients (serves 4)
  • 2 lbs chicken breasts (leftover from Monday works great here)
  • 1 English cucumber, chopped
  • 2 cups cherry tomatoes, halved
  • ½ red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup kalamata olives, halved
  • 1 cup crumbled feta
  • 5 oz Romaine or spring mix
  • 4 tbsp olive oil, 2 tbsp red wine vinegar, 1 tsp dried oregano, salt, pepper
How to Make
  1. If cooking fresh chicken: season with oregano, salt, olive oil. Grill or pan-sear 6–7 min per side. Rest and slice.
  2. Whisk olive oil, red wine vinegar, oregano, salt, pepper into a simple vinaigrette.
  3. Combine cucumber, tomatoes, red onion, olives in a bowl. Toss with half the dressing. Let sit 5 min.
  4. Arrange greens, top with marinated veggies, sliced chicken, and crumbled feta.
  5. Drizzle remaining dressing. Serve with warm pita bread if desired.

Sunday
Cobb Salad
The original hearty salad — hard-boiled eggs, bacon, chicken, avocado
30 minPrep
~540Cal / serving
48gProtein
Ingredients (serves 4)
  • 1½ lbs cooked chicken breast (use leftovers or rotisserie)
  • 6 large eggs
  • 6 strips thick-cut bacon
  • 2 heads Romaine, chopped
  • 2 avocados, diced
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • ½ cup blue cheese crumbles (or feta)
  • ½ cup ranch or blue cheese dressing
How to Make
  1. Hard boil eggs: cover with cold water, bring to boil, cook 10 min, transfer to ice water. Peel and slice.
  2. Cook bacon until crisp. Crumble when cool.
  3. Dice or shred chicken. Dice avocado right before serving (toss with a little lemon juice to prevent browning).
  4. Arrange Romaine in a wide bowl or platter. Lay toppings in rows — classic Cobb style — or just pile it on.
  5. Drizzle dressing over top. Serve immediately.
Nutrition Estimate
~540 cal
48g protein
34g fat
14g carbs

The key to making this week work without daily effort: do a 30-minute Sunday prep session. Wash and dry greens, cook a batch of chicken, hard-boil eggs, and roast sweet potatoes. That front-loads the work so Mon–Wed nights are genuinely fast.

Sunday Batch Prep
Do this before the week starts — it makes everything easier
~30–35 min
🔪
Wash and dry all lettuce — Both Romaine heads and the bag of arugula/spring mix. Spin in a salad spinner or pat completely dry with paper towels. Store in a large zip-lock bag with a paper towel inside to absorb moisture. Lasts all week.
🍗
Cook one big batch of chicken breasts — Season 3 lbs of breasts simply with salt, pepper, garlic powder. Bake at 400°F for 22–25 min or pan-sear. Slice and refrigerate. Covers Monday's Caesar and provides backup for Saturday's Greek if you don't feel like cooking again.
🥚
Hard boil 6 eggs for Sunday's Cobb — You can do this ahead and refrigerate unpeeled for up to a week. Peel night of.
🍠
Roast sweet potatoes — Cube and roast all sweet potato at once (Tuesday's salad). Keeps in fridge 4 days and reheats in 2 min in a skillet.
🧅
Slice red onions — Slice both red onions thin, store in a jar with water and a splash of red wine vinegar. Mellows the bite, keeps all week, and works in the Greek and Cobb salads.
🫙
Mix dressings that keep — Make the lemon-dill vinaigrette (Thursday) and the Greek vinaigrette (Saturday) now. Store in small jars in the fridge. Shake before using.
Weeknight Order of Operations
How to get dinner on the table in 20–25 min on a busy night
20–25 min
1️⃣
Start protein first, always. The moment you walk in, get the protein going — chicken on the grill, steak in the pan, ground beef browning. Everything else takes less time.
2️⃣
Chop while protein cooks. Use the cook time to dice avocado, slice cucumber, halve tomatoes, crumble cheese. Keep the cutting board busy.
3️⃣
Build the base while protein rests. Protein needs 5–8 min to rest. Use that time to lay the greens, arrange toppings, and mix any dressing you haven't prepped.
4️⃣
Slice protein last, right before serving. This keeps it juicy and hot at the table.

🥬 Keeping Greens Fresh All Week
  • Wash and spin dry all greens on Sunday before storing.
  • Store with a dry paper towel in a zip-lock bag — the towel absorbs moisture and keeps greens crisp 5–6 days.
  • Never dress greens ahead of time — always dress right before serving.
  • Arugula wilts faster than Romaine. Use it by Thursday.
  • If greens start to go limp, soak in ice water for 5 min — they'll crisp right back up.
💪 Exercise-Aligned Nutrition
  • Every salad here hits 40–50g protein per serving — well above the minimum for muscle recovery.
  • On heavy lift days (Mon, Tue, Thu) lean into the higher-carb salads: Taco, Steak with sweet potato.
  • Add a second chicken breast or extra egg to any salad to bump protein without changing the recipe.
  • Dressings add fat (and calories) — measure if you're tracking, pour freely if you're not.
  • Avocado in the Cobb is quality fat — leave it in, it supports hormone production and recovery.
🛒 Shopping Smart for Eureka
  • Costco first, Macey's second on the same trip — Costco is on the way back from Spanish Fork.
  • Buy avocados firm at the start of the week — they'll ripen perfectly by Wednesday–Sunday.
  • Rotisserie chicken from Macey's or Costco is a completely valid swap for any recipe — saves 25 min.
  • All canned goods (beans, corn, olives) and bottled dressings keep indefinitely — stock up so future weeks need fewer stops.
  • Frozen salmon works perfectly fine if the fresh counter is picked over.
🔄 Swaps & Variations
  • Don't eat fish? Swap Thursday's salmon for more chicken thighs.
  • Want more carbs? Add a cup of cooked quinoa or brown rice to any salad as a base layer.
  • Avocados not ripe? The Cobb works fine without — or sub in extra bacon.
  • Kids not into salad? The taco and BBQ chicken toppings work as taco bowls or wraps with the same ingredients.
  • Repeat a favorite: if Wednesday's Taco Salad was a hit, there's enough beef for a second batch Friday.
