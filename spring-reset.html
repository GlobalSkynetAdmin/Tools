🌿 Weed Strategy — No Lawn, Gravel & Dirt
You don't need to win the war all at once. The goal is to knock back the worst offenders now while the dumpster is here, then keep on top of it with low-cost methods through spring and summer. Hit them early before they seed — one weed that seeds means hundreds next year.
🧪 Vinegar + Salt Spray Recipe
Mix: 1 gallon white vinegar + 1 cup table salt + 1 tbsp dish soap

Spray directly on weeds on a sunny day — the sun amplifies the kill. Works best on young weeds. Do not spray near anything you want to keep — this kills indiscriminately and can affect soil for a season.

Cost: Under $5 per gallon. Works within 24–48 hrs. Re-apply after rain.
🔥 Torch Notes
Propane torch works well on gravel driveways and rock areas where you can't easily spray. Check wind conditions first. Best in early morning when everything is still slightly damp. Don't torch during dry spell warnings — Utah spring can get dry fast. Hit the base of the plant, not the top.
💧 Boiling Water
Best for weeds coming up through gravel near the house foundation or in tight spots where a torch is too risky. Boil a full kettle, pour directly onto the root zone. Kills on contact. Free. Works in two pours for established roots.
