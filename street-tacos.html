Chunga's-inspired al pastor — sweet citrus marinade, high-heat char, creamy green salsa. Choose your protein below.

Protein
Boneless thighs · leaner · quick cook
Serves 4 12 tacos
0 / 6 steps done
The Method
1
Toast & soak the chilies
Dry-toast 5 guajillo chilies in a pan over medium heat, pressing down for about 30 seconds per side until fragrant. Remove stems and seeds, then soak in boiling water for 20 minutes until softened.
⏱ 20 min soak
2
Blend the marinade
Blend soaked guajillos, 2 tbsp achiote paste, ½ cup canned pineapple juice, ¼ cup OJ, 2 tbsp honey or agave, ¼ cup apple cider vinegar, 5 garlic cloves, half a white onion, 2 chipotles in adobo, 1 tsp cumin, 1 tsp Mexican oregano, and a pinch of ground cloves. Blend until silky. Strain if needed.
Canned pineapple juice only — fresh bromelain will turn chicken mushy.
Fresh or canned pineapple juice both work for pork — the fat content handles the enzymes.
3
Slice & marinate the chicken
Slice & marinate the pork
Slice 2 lbs boneless skinless chicken thighs into 1-inch strips. Coat thoroughly in marinade. Refrigerate at least 4 hours — overnight (up to 18 hrs) is ideal.
Slice 2 lbs pork shoulder (pork butt) into ½-inch slabs or bite-sized chunks, cutting against the grain. Coat thoroughly in marinade. Refrigerate at least 6 hours — overnight (up to 24 hrs) is ideal for maximum penetration.
⏱ 4–18 hrs
4
High-heat cook
High-heat cook
Oven: Preheat to 425°F. Spread chicken and 1 cup pineapple chunks on a sheet pan in a single layer. Roast 20–22 min, then broil 3–5 min to char the edges.

Cast iron: Sear in batches over high heat to 165°F internal. Chop into small pieces, return to pan with a splash of reserved marinade for a quick glaze.
Oven: Preheat to 425°F. Spread pork and 1 cup pineapple chunks on a sheet pan. Roast 25–30 min, then broil 4–6 min until edges char and fat crisps.

Cast iron: Sear pork chunks in batches over high heat — pork needs more time than chicken, 4–5 min per side, to render the fat and get real char. Chop and glaze with reserved marinade.
⏱ ~25 min
The broil is not optional — that's where the trompo char comes from.
Don't rush the fat rendering. Crispy pork fat edges = the whole point.
5
Char the tortillas
Warm small corn tortillas directly on a gas flame or dry griddle until slightly charred on each side. Keep warm in a folded towel.
⏱ ~1 min per tortilla
6
Assemble
Layer meat and a chunk of grilled pineapple on each tortilla. Top with finely diced white onion, fresh cilantro, a squeeze of lime, and a spoonful of creamy green salsa.

Chicken Notes
Thighs, not breasts. They hold up to high heat without drying out.
Canned pineapple juice only in the marinade. Fresh pineapple has bromelain — it'll turn chicken chalky if left too long. Fresh pineapple is fine as a garnish.
Don't skip the broil. That final 3–5 min is what mimics the trompo char.
Pork shoulder preferred. High fat + connective tissue = thermal resilience. It won't dry out on you.
Fresh or canned juice both work for pork — the fat content handles the bromelain.
Don't rush the fat. Pork al pastor lives or dies on rendered, crisped fat edges. Give it the heat it needs.
Longer marinate = better. 12–24 hours lets the achiote and chili really saturate the meat.
